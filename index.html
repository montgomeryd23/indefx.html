Net Neutrality

When and how should internet service providers be allowed to treat
some kinds of internet traffic different from others?

Net neutrality is when internet service providers (ISPs) treat all users on their network
the same, without discriminating or charging differently. Net neutrality means that an
internet service provider, like Verizon or AT&T, has to allow access to all sites at the
same speed, and condition, rather than making users pay a premium to access some
sites, or slowing down internet speed, or barring certain sites.

Impacted Groups

Everyone who uses the internet is affected because without net neutrality people can be
discriminated against or charged differently because of who they are, the website
they are accessing, the platform they are on, the application they are viewing, their
source address, destination address, or device they are using. Without net neutrality
you may not be able to use your favorite apps and services, or may have to suffer through
lower quality and slower connections on your favorite platforms.

Internet content providers, like Facebook and Netflix, love net neutrality because despite
taking up more bandwidth, ISPs cannot charge them more than others for all the data
they are transporting. These are charged the same despite moving bigger packets of
data and can make more money. Since net neutrality benefits these companies that use
more bandwidth, Netflix and Spotify voiced their concerns over net neutrality
possibly coming to an end. These companies also favor net neutrality because
their services can’t be blocked or slowed down by the ISPs.

On the contrary, ISPs like Verizon don’t like net neutrality because they can’t
charge these companies extra for using more bandwidth. Without net neutrality
the ISPs could pick and choose which companies to block and slow down.
They could charge companies more money to reach viewers smoothly and therefore
make more money. ISPs could also slow down other services in order to promote
their own rival services. For instance, AT&T could slow down or block Netflix
in order to promote DirecTV which they own.

Recommendations

Currently we have net neutrality but it is not official; I suggest that we make it official.
Not only does net neutrality also ban ISPs from favoring their own movies and TV shows
and charging more and hurting their rivals like Netflix, YouTube, but it also bans ISPs
from hurting startups that haven’t formed yet. Net neutrality allows for an equal chance
for new companies to form and be successful. Without net neutrality ISPs can slow down
the websites or services of small businesses that can’t afford the faster connections
and consequently derail startup businesses. This will decrease innovation and growth
as there will be no new companies. This will result in the decline of motivation
to innovate, considering there will be no competition.
